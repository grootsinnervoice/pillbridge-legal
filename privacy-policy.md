# Privacy Policy for PillBridge

**Last Updated: April 9, 2026**

PillBridge ("we," "our," or "the app") is a medication tracking application that helps users manage their medications and allows designated caregivers to monitor adherence. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information.

## 1. Information We Collect

When you use PillBridge, we collect the following types of information:

**Account Information**
- Email address
- Full name
- Phone number (optional)
- Password (stored securely by Firebase Authentication and never accessible to us in plain text)
- Account type (Patient or Caregiver)

**Medication Information**
- Medication names, dosages, frequencies, and scheduled times
- Pill counts and refill history (if refill tracking is enabled)
- Photos of medications (if you choose to add them)
- History of doses marked as taken, including timestamps

**Care Group Information**
- Unique care codes linking patients and caregivers
- Relationships between patient and caregiver accounts

**Device Information**
- Push notification tokens (used to deliver medication reminders and caregiver alerts)
- Operating system (iOS or Android)

We do **not** collect location data, contacts, browsing history, or advertising identifiers.

## 2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

- Provide core app functionality: tracking medications, sending reminders, and calculating adherence statistics
- Enable the caregiver monitoring feature by sharing medication and adherence data between patients and their explicitly linked caregivers
- Send local notifications to remind you to take your medications
- Send push notifications to caregivers when relevant events occur (e.g., missed doses)
- Display refill warnings when pill counts run low

We do **not** use your information for advertising, analytics, profiling, or any commercial purpose beyond providing the app.

## 3. How Your Information Is Shared

**With Caregivers You Authorize:** When you share your unique 6-character care code with a caregiver, they gain the ability to view your medications, adherence history, and related health data within the app. You can revoke this access at any time by having the caregiver stop monitoring you, or by generating a new care code.

**With Service Providers:** We use Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore, Cloud Storage, and Cloud Functions) to operate PillBridge. Your data is stored on Google's secure servers in accordance with Google's privacy and security standards. We do not sell, rent, or share your data with any other third parties.

**Legal Requirements:** We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or court order.

## 4. Data Storage and Security

- All data is stored on Google Firebase infrastructure with industry-standard encryption in transit and at rest.
- Passwords are handled by Firebase Authentication and are never stored by PillBridge in plain text.
- Access to your data is restricted to your own account and any caregivers you have explicitly linked via care code.

## 5. Your Rights and Choices

You have the right to:

- **Access** the data stored about you by opening the app
- **Edit or delete** any medication, profile, or care group information at any time within the app
- **Delete your account** entirely by contacting us at the email below. This will permanently remove your account, medications, dose history, photos, and care group links.
- **Revoke caregiver access** at any time
- **Disable notifications** via your device settings

## 6. Children's Privacy

PillBridge is not intended for use by children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13. If you believe we have collected information from a child, please contact us and we will promptly delete it.

## 7. Health Information Disclaimer

PillBridge is a self-management tool and is **not** a medical device. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional regarding your medications. PillBridge is not a HIPAA-covered entity and should not be used as a substitute for professional medical record-keeping systems.

## 8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this document. Significant changes will be communicated in-app.
